The short version

Reading systems, then writing them clearly.

I read English at the University of Dhaka before I learned that what I really wanted was to report. Six years among novels, theory, and long arguments over language did not move me away from the practical world. They gave me a sharper way to enter it.

That habit moved with me into newsrooms and research. I write about the systems behind what people think they know: algorithmic feeds, misinformation cascades, platform power, evidence trails, and the institutions that shape public attention.

I teach, too. Sometimes inside classrooms, sometimes through workshops, and sometimes by designing learning systems for myself and others. The recurring interest is the same: help people see structure, ask better questions, and leave with something usable.

The rest of this site is the record: what I have written, what I am researching, where I have taught, and the quieter room that lives beyond the work.
